1.  What is the aim of holistic assessment?

<!-- -->

1.  To develop a problem list of all of the child’s needs and develop a care plan specifically for that child and family

2.  To decide whether the family are capable of looking after the child

3.  To make a decision as to what information needs to be shared with the family about the child’s schooling needs while in hospital

4.  To help the doctor plan what medication the child requires to make a full recovery

<!-- -->

1.  A home visit

2.  Time, good communication skills, empathy and a non-judgemental attitude

3.  A checklist to be completed

4.  The doctor to make the final decisions about the interventions to be implemented

<!-- -->

1.  A verbal agreement between healthcare providers

<!-- -->

1.  A palliative care plan and a nursing care plan

2.  A palliative care plan, an advance care plan and a terminal plan

<!-- -->

1.  Who develops the palliative care plan?

<!-- -->

1.  The doctor in charge

2.  The palliative care nurse

3.  The team of healthcare providers who are actively involved in the care including the child and family

4.  The doctor, nurse and social worker but not the family

<!-- -->

1.  When should a palliative care plan be developed?

<!-- -->

1.  When the patient’s condition starts deteriorating

2.  As soon as possible after the diagnosis is made

3.  On the day the patient is admitted to hospital

4.  When the patient reaches the terminal stage

<!-- -->

1.  The palliative care plan identifies;

<!-- -->

1.  The problems, the action planned, the person responsible and the review timeframe

2.  The family’s involvement in the plan

3.  A checklist of problems and actions only

4.  The expected outcomes of treatment

> 19\. The advance care plan is drawn up?

1.  At the time of diagnosis

2.  When the child and family have given up hope

3.  When there are indications that the disease is getting worse despite treatments or that the end-of-life phase has been entered

4.  By the doctor once he has told the family that there is nothing more that can be done for the child

1.  What is a terminal care plan?

<!-- -->

1.  A terminal care plan will help guide the care and interventions to be provided in the last few hours and immediately after the death of the child

2.  It is another name for an advance care plan

3.  A terminal care plan is a legal medical document that needs to be completed when the child has died

4.  A terminal care plan lists the medication that needs to be prescribed at the end-of-life only
