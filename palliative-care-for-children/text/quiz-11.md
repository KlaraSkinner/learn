**b. It is easier than in an adult**

**6. Indicators of impending death include: **

1.  **Increased interaction and playfulness**

2.  **Passing lots of urine**

3.  **Clingy behaviour**

4.  **Stable vital signs**

**7. What is important for a ‘good death’?**

1.  **Delaying death until everyone is ready**

2.  **The focus is on the dying child**

3.  **The focus is on the surviving family members**

4.  **A quick and sudden death reduces staffing demands**

**8. The place of death for a child depends on:**

1.  **The wishes of the family and available resources**

2.  **The provincial policy**

3.  **The choice of the healthcare team**

4.  **The reason the child is dying**

**9. . When discussing the child’s death with the family:**

**a. The doctor’s religion should decide what needs to be done**

**b. The hospital rules must always be followed even if the family disagree**

**c. All families need a lot of guidance and instruction**

**d. Health carers should work sensitively, at the family’s pace**

**10. An advance care plan:**

1.  **Is strictly confidential**

2.  **Should be drawn up when the child is dying**

3.  **Should be drawn up in a time of calm**

4.  **Should not be modified at any time**

**11. What is a terminal care plan?**

1.  **A plan to stop all symptom management**

2.  **A plan that deals specifically with end-of-life concerns and issues**

3.  **Assessing the cost of hospitalisation**

4.  **A plan that is used before the dying phase**

**12. The main aim of end-of-life symptom control is to:**

1.  **Speed up the dying process**

2.  **Keep the child awake and aware**

3.  **Minimise the child’s pain and fear**

4.  **Prolong life for as long as possible**

**13. When managing pain at the end-of-life:**

1.  **There is no maximum dose of opioids**

2.  **Opioids are to be used only as a last option**

3.  **Opioids commonly cause respiratory depression**

4.  **The morphine dose should be increased slowly**

**14. What does the management of shortness of breath at the end-of-life include?**

1.  **Intubation and ventilation**

2.  **Oxygen via a face mask**

3.  **Ensuring the child lies flat in bed**

4.  **Opening a window, or directing a fan towards the child**

**15. Restlessness and agitation:**

1.  **Could be due to constipation or urinary retention**

2.  **Indicates psychiatric problems**

3.  **Should be conservatively managed without sedation**

4.  **Should only be managed with morphine**

**16. Airway secretions at the end-of-life: **

1.  **Are very distressing to the patient**

2.  **Should be managed with increased intravenous fluids**

3.  **Can be treated with hyoscine butylbromide (Buscopan)**

4.  **Should be regularly suctioned**

**17. Seizures at the end-of-life: **

1.  **Should always be treated in hospital**

2.  **Buccal midazolam (Dormicum) can be given at home**

3.  **Underlying causes should be ignored**

4.  **Rarely happen, so it is wasteful to prepare for them**

**18. Massive haemorrhage at the end-of-life:**

1.  **Can be managed with white towels**

2.  **Is generally not distressing**

3.  **Is managed with an emergency blood transfusion**

4.  **Sedation should be given if the child is awake and afraid**

**19. When providing fluid and food at the end-of-life:**

1.  **Only give food if the child is hungry**

2.  **Ensure the child is fed, by whichever route is best**

3.  **Intravenous fluids should be a given rather than oral fluids**

4.  **Supplements still play an important role**

## What information does the family need to know that death has occurred?

1.  **That the child has stopped moving both arms and legs**

2.  **There is no breath, heartbeat and pupils will be fixed**

3.  **There may still be a few gasps of air even though the heart has stopped beating**

4.  **Rigor mortis will start immediately**
