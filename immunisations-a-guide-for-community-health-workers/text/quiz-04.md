# Quiz 4: Immunisation risks

Please choose the one, most correct answer to each question or statement.

1.  What is the greatest immunisation risk?

a.  Swallowing the oral polio vaccine
b.  Giving the injection on the wrong side of the body
c.  Not being fully immunised

2.  How many children are fully immunised in South Africa?

a.  About 80%
b.  About 70%
c,  About 50%

3.  Why are some children not fully immunised?

a.  It is too expensive
b.  Lack of vaccines at the clinic
c.  The mother is afraid that the injection will be painful

4.  Is it safe to immunise malnourished children?

a.  Yes
b.  Only if they have started to regain weight
c.  No

5.  Is it safe to immunise children who have a cough and runny nose?

a.  Yes
b.  Only if they are older than 18 months
c.  No

6.  What is a common side effect of immunisation?

a.  Diarrhoea
b.  Redness, swelling and tenderness at the site of the injection
c.  Weakness of the leg or arm

7.  It is common at the site of BCG immunisation to have:

a.  A large bruise
b.  A small ulcer
c.  Bleeding

8.  Why is a smaller dose of diphtheria vaccine used after 18 months?

a.  Because it is cheaper
b.  Because it is less painful
c.  Because the risk of side effects is increased if a full dose is used

9.  Should infants of HIV positive mothers be immunised?

a.  Yes
b.  Only if they are first given antiretroviral treatment (ART)
c.  No

10. What is a cold chain?

a.  A common complication of Rota virus immunisation
b.  A method to keep vaccines cool when they are taken out of the fridge
c.  A bangle to prevent pain after BCG immunisation
